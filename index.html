01 / About

Background

I'm a software engineer with a deep background in systems programming, cloud infrastructure, and network virtualization. I thrive at the intersection of low-level systems and modern distributed architectures.

My career started at Alcatel-Lucent where I worked on wireless radio access networks — writing kernel modules, FPGA drivers, and platform software for next-generation LTE infrastructure. That foundation in embedded systems and networking shaped how I think about building reliable, performant software.

At VMware I worked on Network Security Virtualization, helping architect software-defined networking solutions that abstract and secure infrastructure at scale. I pursued my graduate degree at USC concurrently, deepening my understanding of distributed systems, algorithms, and computer networks.

I'm passionate about building things that work at scale and writing about technology, systems design, and ideas on my Views blog.
